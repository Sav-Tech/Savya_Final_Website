The Rise and Fall of Elizabeth Holmes

Elizabeth Holmes is facing federal charges for massive fraud. She is the founder of Theranos (a 9 billion dollar company). She was a dropout from Stanford University and was about to get married the day before she was arrested. Holmes is going to be faced with a trial in August 2020 and could be convicted 20 years in prison. She was a master in lying as she convinced many people such as Joe Biden, Barack Obama, Jack Ma, and even Bill Clinton.

She had a dream of being a billionaire when she was just 9 years old! Most of the people hadn't decided their life ahead when they were just 9 years old, but young Elizabeth had everything clear in her mind. Just like all the other famous businessmen she also started in Silicon Valley, California. Her goal was to make a little device that can run more than 100 blood tests with a little bit of blood. Her vision was very good as this could help a lot of people. Earlier just to take one test people would have to go to the doctor and give a lot of their blood away. Then the doctor would take it and do a lot of tests in their lab. This process would take thousands of dollars but the Elizabeths device you do it quickly and just in 2.99 dollars. Many people even considered her as the next Steve Jobs. To make an impression, she dressed like Steve Jobs and changed her voice. Her idea was brilliant but when she realized that her company could not make the device she started to fraud people. She told the world that this device works when it was actually faulty. This was very dangerous for people as if they get the wrong info they might take more/less of what they need and then eventually die. To secure her secret, Elizabeth tracked all of her employees both physically and digitally. She had to make sure that nobody tells the world the truth about her. Although three people figured it out and were going to tell the press when Elizabeth charged them for trading Theranos's secrets. This was legal as on the contract that the employees signed they were not allowed to tell anyone about Theranos. She convinced the judge and sentenced them to prison.
